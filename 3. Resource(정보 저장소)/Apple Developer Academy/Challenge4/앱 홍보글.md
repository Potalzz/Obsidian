## English Version

🚀 **visionOS App Escaple Launch!**

Excited to announce that **Escaple**, a visionOS escape room app developed with an incredible team of 5 over **6 weeks** at **Apple Developer Academy@POSTECH**, is now **live on the App Store**! 🎉

The first two weeks were filled with continuous brainstorming sessions. We immersed ourselves in understanding the unique characteristics of visionOS as a new platform, researching and iterating through countless ideas to identify the most suitable concept.

Among numerous concepts, the escape room genre perfectly aligned with our team's criteria. It offered a truly differentiated experience that only Vision Pro could deliver while providing an ideal playground to experiment with visionOS's diverse capabilities. Most importantly, it was a theme that ignited genuine passion in every team member.

During development, we made a fascinating technical discovery. While initially considering Full Immersive environments, we found that the actual spatial constraints were smaller than expected—approximately a 1-meter radius. This led us to implement **Mixed Reality** instead, allowing us to utilize users' actual space while maximizing immersion.

Escaple isn't just another puzzle-solving escape room game. We invested significant time crafting compelling narratives that leverage Vision Pro's unique interactions and Mixed Reality advantages, creating an experience so immersive that users truly feel trapped in a real room. This project has been transformational for my development journey. Surprisingly, my undergraduate background—which seemed distant from coding when I started development two years ago—became an invaluable asset.

My knowledge of 3D pipelines and hands-on VFX experience in film production served as a crucial bridge between our development and design teams. This unique background allowed me to contribute to development while simultaneously connecting both teams, significantly strengthening our collaborative workflow.

Taking on a new platform was certainly not an easy decision, but seeing our collective work reach real users in such a short timeframe fills me with immense pride and satisfaction.

I'm excited to continue growing as a developer in the Spatial Computing space, constantly pushing boundaries and exploring new possibilities.
